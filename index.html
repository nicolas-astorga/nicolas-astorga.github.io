About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Machine Learning at the University of Cambridge (van der Schaar Lab). My work focuses on enhancing the reasoning capabilities of large language models through post-training techniques, with prior work at the intersection of LLMs, Bayesian experimental design, and optimisation. My research has appeared in NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR (Spotlight), ECCV, and AISTATS.

Supervisor: Prof. Mihaela van der Schaar · Fully funded by the W.D. Armstrong Trust Fund award · Expected graduation: 2027.

See all publications Google Scholar GitHub

Current Research

  • Reasoning at scale: synthetic data and curriculum-style pipelines to improve reasoning in LLMs.
  • Autoformulation (ICML 2025): MCTS + LLMs for automated formulation of optimisation-modelling problems.
  • Efficient Q&A with LLMs (ICLR 2025): active task disambiguation in LLMs using BED acquisition metrics.
  • Active learning with LLMs (NeurIPS 2024): joint acquisition of labels and features using LLM surrogate models trained with SFT.
  • LLMs for Bayesian Optimisation (ICLR 2024): LLAMBO to leverage LLMs within BO pipelines.
